Is a gameboy an instrument?

Yes, yes it is. I don't just mean ost's to gameboy games, I mean songs that are composed on gameboy hardware, or use soundfonts similar to that of a gameboy. They're called chiptunes

One of my favorite chiptune albums is Parellel Processing by Danimal Cannon and Zef.

One of the common pitfalls of chiptune music is that songs from inexperienced artists can sound a little empty due to the limited hardware. While many artists get around this by adding extra instruments or synths, Parellel Processing avoids this by being mostly composed on two gameboys rather than just one. The result is an album jam packed with catchy melodies and hard-hitting basslines that most gameboy games from back in the day wished they had.

  1. Logic Gatekeeper

    My favorite track on the album. It perfectly captures the spirit of Parallel Processing and serves as a great intro for what the album is entails, with fast-paced, punchy melodies at both ends tied together seamlessly with a middle section that is more subdued but still retains the energy of the rest of the track.

  2. Legacy

    This was the first song I ever heard from Danimal Cannon, as it was featured in the trailer for the Pokemon Generations youtube series, and for good reason. The slower tempo of this piece combined with the low melody and steady percussion gives this piece a heroic vibe, like you're approaching the end of a grand adventure.

  3. Chronos

    Were the previous tracks not dubstep-y enough for you? Because this song is probably the closest you can get on a gameboy. It's pure ear candy, and I find it's near impossible to not tap my foot to the beat when the bass kicks in, but it may not be everyone's cup of tea.

  4. Rhapsody

    I don't really have a whole lot to say about this one. It's more melodic and happy than the rest of the album, and while I don't think it stands out that much, it still has that great Danimal Cannon vibe.

  5. Glitch

    This one is Zef's solo track on the album, and his style definitely helps this one stand out. It's very fast paced and kind of all over the place. While this means it's not super memorable, it is still fun, and it's got enough variety in it to keep it interesting throughout.

  6. The Lunar Whale

    This one's a Danimal Cannon solo track, and it's one of the best. Some parts are very punchy and wild, some parts are very melodic, and all parts are equally memorable and fun to listen to. Also, I can't not bring up the part at 00:42, the rhythm is so weird but it's so fun to listen to; it's definitely the part of the song that I think of the most.

  7. Corrupted

    After Danimal Cannon and Zef both get their solo tracks, they come back together for Corrupted, which is the most thematically interesting track on the album. It starts with a simple melody, then continues to add on it for two minutes before building up to a very satisfying drop with a fantastic melody, and satisfying drops are, I find, pretty rare in chiptune music. The second half of the song even uses some compressed voice samples, which helps this track stand out even more

  8. Parallel Processing

    Sharing the same name, I think this track is a great finale for the album, much like how Logic Gatekeeper is a great intro. It's really long, at 8 minutes, which allows it to have a lot of variety throughout, with calm parts and heavier parts, capturing the essence of the whole album. Despite this however, I feel like this track gets heavily overshadowed by the big hitters of the album, especially Corrupted, as it came just before. Very ironic, given its name.
